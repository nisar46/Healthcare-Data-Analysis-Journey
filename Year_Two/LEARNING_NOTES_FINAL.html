💰 Learning Notes: Year 2 - The Billing Audit

🚀 Project Context: Revenue Protection

WHAT did we do?
We built an "Auditor Bot" that scans every single bill to find money leaks (unbilled visits) or overcharges (extra zeros).

WHY did we do it?
Small typos cost money. A negative bill (`-500`) or a zero bill means the hospital loses revenue. Humans miss these in large Excel sheets.

HOW did we do it?
We used "Loops" to go through every row, and "Comparisons" (`>`, `<`) to flag numbers that looked suspicious.

💡 LESSON LEARNT:
Data isn't just medical; it's financial. Python can be a strict Accountant that never gets tired.

Topic: Finding lost money using Python (Loops and Conditions)

In Year 2, you moved to the Billing Department. You noticed small mistakes were causing huge losses. We used Python to "audit" every single bill instantly.

1. The "Detective Work" (The Logic)

We are looking for three specific money leaks:

  1. Negative Bills: Why is the hospital paying the patient? (Typing -500 instead of 500).
  2. Extra Zeros: Charging a patient 50,000 instead of 5,000.
  3. Zero Bills: Patients leaving without being charged at all.

2. The "Receipt Check" (The Loop)

Think of a Grocery Store Checkout.
The code is the scanner. It picks up one item (row) at a time, checks the price tag, and decides if it makes sense. 
billing_issues = []
for row in reader:
    bill = float(row['Bill_Amount'])
  • for row in reader:: "Next customer, please!" This loop goes through every single patient one by one.
  • float(...): Converting text into a "Number" so we can do math.

3. The "Rules" (The If-Statements)

Here are the safety checks we installed at the checkout counter:

A. The "Fat Finger" Check (Too High)

if bill > 50000:
    issue = "Potential Extra Zero Error"

Translation: "If the price is huge, flag it!" Unless it's a major surgery, an OPD bill shouldn't be this high.

B. The "Reverse Money" Check (Negative)

elif bill < 0:
    issue = "Negative Bill Error"

Translation: "If the number is less than zero, that's impossible."

C. The "Freebie" Check (Zero)

elif bill == 0:
    issue = "Unbilled Visit"

Translation: "Did they forget to pay?"

4. The "Bucket" (Collecting Errors)

if issue:
    billing_issues.append(row)
  • append: This just means "Add to list". Imagine you have a red basket for "Problem Items". If a bill has an issue, you throw it in the red basket to check later.

💡 Key Takeaway

You taught Python to be a Financial Auditor. Instead of staring at 1,000 receipts, you wrote 10 lines of code that filtered out only the bad ones instantly.
