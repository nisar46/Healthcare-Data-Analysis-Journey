🚀 Project Context: Digital Governance

WHAT did we do?
We audited our database to see if we were ready for the new DPDPA (Privacy) Laws and ABDM integration.

WHY did we do it?
Non-compliance leads to fines. We needed to prove our data was "Clean" (No PII leaks) and "Linked" (Valid ABHA IDs).

HOW did we do it?
We used "Multi-Condition Checks" (`and` logic) and "Thresholds". We set a passing score (70%) to grade our own performance.

💡 LESSON LEARNT:
Code is Law. You used Python not just to fix errors, but to enforce a standard of quality across the entire hospital.

Topic: Percentages & Thresholds (The "Pass/Fail" Test)

In Year 5, your role shifted to Governance. The new law (DPDPA 2023) required data to be clean and linked to ABHA IDs. You wrote a script to "Audit" the hospital's readiness.

1. The "Safety Inspector" (The Concept)

Think of a Car Safety Inspection.
You check every car (patient record).
1. Are the brakes working? (Is the name clean/no typos?)
2. Is the seatbelt on? (Do they have an ABHA ID?)
If YES, they get a "Pass" sticker.

2. The "Checklist" (The Conditions)

if "@" not in row['Patient_Name'] and row['ABHA_ID'] != "":
    compliance_score += 1
  • and: This means BOTH things must be true.
    • Is the name clean? (No "@" symbols)
    • AND is the ID box NOT empty?
  • If both are true, we add +1 to our score.

3. The "Scorecard" (The Math)

percentage = (compliance_score / total_checked) * 100

This is simple school math. (Good ones / Total ones) * 100 gives you the score.

The "Red Line" (Threshold)

if percentage < 70:
    print("STATUS: CRITICAL RISK")
else:
    print("STATUS: READY")

We set a "Red Line" at 70%. If the hospital scores below this, we sound the alarm. This is how software makes decisions automatically.

💡 Key Takeaway

This script represents the future of your career. It's not just about fixing one name; it's about Measuring Trust. You are no longer just cleaning data; you are auditing the entire system's health.
