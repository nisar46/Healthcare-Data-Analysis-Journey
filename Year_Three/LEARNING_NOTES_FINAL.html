WHAT did we do?
We identified pregnant patients who came for checkups (OPD) but went to other hospitals for delivery (IP).

Topic: Working with Dates (Delivery Dates) and Lists

In Year 3, you solved a "Leakage" problem. Pregnant women came for checkups (OPD) but went to other hospitals for delivery (IP). You used Python to predict their delivery date and check if they stayed.

1. The "Crystal Ball" (Calculating Dates)

Computers don't know that "January" comes before "February". We have to teach them.

from datetime import datetime, timedelta
edd_date = lmp_date + timedelta(days=280)
  • datetime: A tool that teaches Python clocks and calendars.
  • timedelta: A tool for "Time Travel". Here, we travel forward 280 days (9 months) from their Last Menstrual Period (LMP) to find their Due Date (EDD).

2. The "Party Guest List" (The Logic)

Imagine you are throwing a Huge Wedding.
1. You have a list of people you invited (Pregnant / OPD List).
2. You have a list of people who actually showed up (Admitted / IP List).
3. You want to know: "Who got an invite but didn't show up?"

Step A: Making the Lists

if row['Is_Pregnant'] == "Yes":
    pregnant_patients.append(row)
if row['Visit_Type'] == "IP":
    ip_admissions.append(row['Patient_Name'])

We separate everyone into two groups: "Expecting Moms" and "Admitted Patients".

Step B: Checking Attemptance (The 'In' Operator)

if name in ip_admissions:
    status = "SECURED (Admitted)"
else:
    status = "LEAKAGE RISK (No IP Visit)"
  • in: This is the most English-like word in Python. It literally looks through the ip_admissions list to see if the name is there.
  • If they are NOT in the list, we mark them as a "Risk" — this is money walking out the door!

3. The Result

This script allowed you to print a list of names and say:
"Call these 50 patients today. They are due next month but haven't booked a bed yet!"
